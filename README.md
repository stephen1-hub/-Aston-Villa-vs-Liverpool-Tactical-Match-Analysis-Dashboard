# ⚽ Aston Villa vs Liverpool Tactical Match Analysis Dashboard

## Objective

The objective of this project was to analyze the tactical and attacking performance of Aston Villa and Liverpool during their 2025/2026 Premier League match using Expected Goals (xG) and shot-event data.

The dashboard was designed to:

* Evaluate shot quality versus shot quantity
* Identify the most dangerous attacking players
* Compare open-play and set-piece effectiveness
* Visualize attacking patterns and shooting locations
* Generate tactical insights from match-event data

---

# Data Used

The dataset contained 30 shot events from the match and included:

* Team names
* Player names
* Shot coordinates (X, Y)
* Expected Goals (xG)
* Shot outcomes
* Shot situations
* Shot types

# Key Findings

### 1. Aston Villa Generated Higher-Quality Chances

Although Liverpool attempted more shots overall, Aston Villa produced significantly higher expected goals (xG), indicating better chance quality.

| Team        | Shots | Total xG |
| ----------- | ----: | -------: |
| Aston Villa |    14 |     2.94 |
| Liverpool   |    16 |     1.56 |

---

### 2. Ollie Watkins Was the Main Attacking Threat

Ollie Watkins generated the highest attacking threat in the match with multiple high-xG opportunities from central areas inside the penalty box.

* 6 shots
* 2.16 total xG
* 2 goals

---

### 3. Liverpool Relied Heavily on Set Pieces

Liverpool’s goals mainly came from set-piece situations through Virgil van Dijk, while their open-play chance quality remained relatively low.

---

### 4. Corner Situations Were Extremely Dangerous

Corner situations produced high-value scoring opportunities for both teams, especially Aston Villa.

# Tactical Implications

### Aston Villa

* More efficient attacking structure
* Strong central penalty-box penetration
* High-quality shot selection
* Effective set-piece execution

### Liverpool

* Higher shot volume but lower efficiency
* Relied on aerial and set-piece situations
* Less effective open-play chance creation
